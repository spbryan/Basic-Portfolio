About Me

My journey to a career in IT started on a less traditional path than most. I graduated with a degree in History followed by a Master’s degree in Education. The job market for History teachers was pretty thin at the time but the budding “dot-com boom” and looming “Y2K disaster” created a high demand for IT. I accepted an associate developer position with Liberty Mutual who then provided me with the necessary training

I started my career enhancing the PL1 mainframe Legacy systems in anticipation of Y2K. Once the new millenium arrived, however, and both world and company were still standing, I was moved to a project where all systems were to be rewritten using the latest technology. This provided me with a huge opportunity to work on the design and implementation of a Policy Admin system from the ground up, mastering object-oriented and rules-based technologies such as C++ and Aion. As these systems rolled out and evolved I grew into a role of subject matter expert and ultimately the technical owner of the domain. Eventually the company moved to a web-based, service oriented architecture and so I was designated as technical lead for creating the next generation of PA using Java and WebSphere. Eventually these systems also exceeded their shelf-life and so I am now at the beginning of refactoring to my third generation of PA, breaking down the monolith in favor of microservices and taking advantage of the cloud.

Through it all my family has built a very happy life in coastal New Hampshire. My wife and I have three kids who are now (mostly) grown and out of the house. My son is actually in his final semester at UNH earning a degree in IT. One passion I developed over the years was with martial arts which I spent a number of years both studying and teaching. I also enjoy playing the guitar and often make my dogs howl strumming out what I, at least, think is good music.
